Our story

Built for the food you actually eat.

BetterPlate is a nutrition tracker designed around real Indian meals — dal, roti, sabzi, rice, and everything in between. No Western food bias. No complicated dashboards. No constant upsell prompts.

Why this exists

A bad blood report changes things. Mine did.

Four years of ignoring my lipid profile caught up with me. I was what doctors politely call "skinny fat" — lean on the outside, metabolic chaos on the inside. Six kilos of fat to lose, and finally, the motivation to do something about it.

I tried every calorie tracking app on the Play Store. One had seventeen versions of "dal fry" with wildly different calorie counts. Another had no Indian food at all. The rest were either confusing to navigate or pushed paid upgrades every time I opened them. I just wanted to log my lunch.

So I did what any Excel-addicted professional does — I built a spreadsheet. Columns for food, grams, macros, totals. It worked, technically. I lasted four days before I accepted that nobody should have to do this.

That frustration became BetterPlate.

I realised I wasn't alone. There are millions of young professionals in India trying to eat better — people who grew up on dal-chawal and sabzi-roti, not chicken salads and quinoa bowls. They deserve a nutrition app built around their food, not one that treats Indian cuisine as an afterthought.
